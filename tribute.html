Marie Curie

(1867 - 1934)

Marie Curie with her daughters; Irene and Eve.

Marie Curie was a Polish physicist and chemist, she was probably one of the most famous scientists of her time. Marie was awarded the nobel peace prize in 1903 and she went on to win another in 1911. Marie was born in Warsaw on November 7 1867. She went to Paris to study physics and mathematics where she met her future husband Pierre Curie (professor of physics). They were married in 1895. The Curies worked together investigating radioactivity and building on the work of other scientists. In July 1898 they announced the discovery of a new chemical element called Polonium. At the end of the year they also announced the discovery of another element called Radium . Pierre later passed away in 1906 so Marie took over his teaching post, becoming the first woman to teach at the Sorbonne. She devoted herself to continuing the work they had begun together. She then received a second nobel prize for chemistry in 1911. The curie's research played a fundamental role in the development of x-rays. Below is a time-line of Marie's life:

  • 1867 - Born in Warsaw on 7 November
  • 1891 - Went to Paris to study physics and mathematics at the Sorbonne where she met Pierre Curie
  • 1895 - Married Pierre
  • 1898 - Announce the discovery of Polonium and Radium
  • 1903 - Awarded first nobel prize
  • 1906 - Marie became the first woman to teach at the Sorbonne
  • 1911 - Marie wins second nobel prize
  • 1925-1930 - Marie's health began to deteriorate
  • 1934 - Marie passed away July 4 1934
